Methodology & Caveats

  1. Per-match aggregation. For Declared 4-innings matches, a player can appear twice in the raw batting records for a single match. All season and career figures in this piece collapse those to one innings per match — summing runs and balls, counting a single "not-out" only when every constituent innings was not out.
  2. Why average is downplayed. Under TRCC's retirement rule (9–11 balls mandatory, 25 balls max unless last-man-in), a high average often reflects frequency of reaching the cap rather than durability. Strike rate and 30+ rate are the stronger skill signals.
  3. Season coverage. Trend lines begin at each player's earliest recorded per-match season. S1 2018 exists in career totals but has no per-match rows, so that season does not appear on any chart. The 2020–2021 COVID break means there are no seasons plotted for those years. S1 2019 has limited match coverage and will appear sparse.
  4. Gaps vs. zeros. Where a line dips to zero, the player did not play that season — not that they scored none. Hover points to read the match count.
  5. Top-ten defined by. Total career runs (per Career_Batting tab). Ranked in that order throughout.
